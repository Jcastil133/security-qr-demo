You scanned a QR code to reach this page. In real attacks, QR codes can disguise destinations and push users toward risky actions on mobile devices.

Why am I seeing this?
Trainer note: This is intentionally “login-like” to show how easily users can be nudged into trusting a QR flow. No data is captured or transmitted.

What this is teaching (in 30 seconds)

  • QR codes can hide where you’re going until after you scan.
  • Mobile devices are a top target (QR phishing, SMS phishing, risky links, malicious apps).
  • Security works best as layers: Identity + Device + Network.

Where our solutions fit

  • Identity: Entra ID / SSO / MFA controls who can access.
  • Device: MTD (ex: Ivanti) helps detect mobile threats (QR phishing, risky links/apps).
  • Network: SASE helps enforce secure access and block threats on Wi-Fi or cellular; HSI/router security protects entry points.

Today’s goal

You don’t need to be a security expert. You just need to hear the triggers, ask 2–3 safe questions, and bring the SE in early for qualified opportunities.
